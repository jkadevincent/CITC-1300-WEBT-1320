The History of Basketball

The Birth of the Game

During the winter of 1891, a 31-year-old named James Naismith was the athletic instructor at the International YMCA Training School in Springfield, Massachusetts. Mr. Naismith was in charge of creating new and exciting ways to keep his athletes in top form while waiting for sports that are played during warmer times of the year. This led to the creation of a game that could be playable indoors and with a large number of players at the same time. Naismith wished to recreate a game he and his friends played growing up that included a ball and a horizontal opening for the ball to be tossed through. He asked the school janitor for two square boxes to be used as goals. Instead, the janitor returned with peach baskets. These baskets were nailed on opposite sides of the gymnasuim at a height of ten feet in the air. The class was then divided into teams based on height, Naismith created a rulebook, and the game of "basket ball" was born.

Early Evolution of Basketball

  • 1891 - The game of basketball was created.
  • 1893 - The first iron basketball hoop was introduced and marketed.
  • 1894 - Teams began to play with five players a side.
  • 1894 - The first basketball was made, going away from a soccer ball which was used since the creation of the game.
  • 1895 - The modern scoring system (excluding the three pointer) was introduced.
  • 1895 - Teams were recommended to use a backboard behind the hoop.

The First Game

The first intercollegiate basketball game recorded was played on January 18, 1896 in Iowa City. The game was contested between the University of Chicago and the University of Iowa using the rules listed above. The U of Chicago won in a close one, 15-12, with both teams electing to use their starting five for the entirity of the game. By 1905, colleges had unified to create their own rule committee, thus officially creating the sport of college basketball.
